Aquila

Aquila is a constellation in the northern sky covering an area of 652 square degrees. Its name is Latin for "Eagle", it has 3 stars brighter that the magnitude of 3. Aquila lies in the Summer Triangle, it was one of the 48 constellations listed by the 2nd centery AD. astronomer Ptolemy.
The alpha star, Altair, is a vertex of the Summer Triangle. The best time to see the connstellation is in the summer.
There are three interesting nebulas in Aquila:

  1. NGC 6804 shows a small but bright ring
  2. NGC 6781 which bears some resemblance with the Owl Nebula in Ursa Major
  3. NGC 6751 also known as the Glowing Eye, a planetary nebula (pictured on the right)
