Aquarius

Aquarius is a constellation of the zodiac (a path through our sky), it is located between Capricornus and Pisces covering an area of 980 sqaure degrees. Aquarius was named by the ancient greeks in Latin Aqaurius means "water-carrier". Aquarius is one of the oldest of the named constellations. It was one of the 48 constellations listed by the 2nd centery AD. astronomer Ptolemy.
Aquarius has no bright stars, its 4 brightest stars have a magnitude less than 2. Research has shown that there are several stars inside Aquarius have planetary systems. Twelve planetary systems have been found in Aquarius seince 2013.
Aquarius contains three deep sky objects that are in the Messier catalog: Messier 2, Messier 72, and Messier 73. It also contains 2 nabulas: Saturn Nebula (NGC 7009), and the Helix Nebula (NGC 7293). Aquarius also holds many other galaxies.
