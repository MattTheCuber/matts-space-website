Ursa Major

Ursa Major, the Great Bear, is located in the Quadrant NQ2. For the Northern Hemisphere you can see it most of the months but it is best in the month of April. The seven brightest stars of Ursa Major are known as the Big Dipper in the USA and Canada, the Plough in the United Kingdom, the Grober Wagen in Germany Austria and the Saptarshi in India. 6 of the stars are the 2nd magnitude the center star is the 3rd magnitude.
Inside the Ursa Major there are many galaxies and other things including, Messier 81 (one of the brightest galaxies in the sky) and Messier 82 above the bear's head, and Pinwheel Galaxy (M101), the spiral galaxies Messier 108 and Messier 109 are also found in this constellation, the planetary nebula Owl Nebula (M97) can be found at the bottom of the Big Dipper. The Hubble Deep Field is located to the northeast of Ursae Majoris.





Ursa Minor

Ursa Minor, the Little Bear, is located in the Quadrant NQ3. Ursa Minor covering an area of 256 square degrees. A dwarf Galaxy, located in the area of the constellation. It is around 225,000 light years distant from Earth. The North Star, at the end of the Handle, is the only star that never moves, it only spins around in circles.
