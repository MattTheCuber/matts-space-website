Andromeda

Andromeda is one of the largest constellations covering an area 722 sq. degrees. Andromeda is located north of the celestial equator, it is named Andromeda, daughter of Cassiopeia. Andromeda is best seen in fall evenings in the Northern Hemispher.
Andromeda is over 1,400 times thesize of the full moon, 55% of the size of the largest constellation, Hydra, and over 10 times the size of the smallest constellation, Crux.
Andromeda is acutaly made of 12 stars (unlike these maps). Its brightest star, Alpha Andromedae, is a binary star. Sometimes Andromeda is been counted as a part of Pegasus. Beta Andromedae is a red giant, its color is visible to a human eye. The constellation has an object that is visible to the human eye called: Andromeda Galaxy (M31), the closest spiral galaxy to the Milky Way and one of the brightest. The Andromeda galaxy is visible by a human eye. Several fainter galaxies, M110 and M32 and more distant NGC 891, are near Andromeda. The Blue Snowball Nebula, a planetary nebula, is visible in a telescope, which is in Andromeda.
